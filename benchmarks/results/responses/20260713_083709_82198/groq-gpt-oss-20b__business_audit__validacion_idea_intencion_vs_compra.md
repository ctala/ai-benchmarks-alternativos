## What a “real” validation looks like

| Stage | What you need to prove | Typical metrics |
|-------|-----------------------|-----------------|
| **Market‑fit test** | At least 30 paying customers who renew | NPS ≥ 50, churn ≤ 5 % |
| **Pricing test** | Price elasticity at the target price point | Conversion rate at $30/mo, CAC ≤ $20 (if you plan to acquire organically) |
| **Cost‑structure** | Unit economics are positive | Gross margin ≥ 70 %, LTV/CAC ≥ 3 |
| **Scale potential** | Enough potential users to sustain growth | TAM ≥ $X, SAM ≥ $Y (based on your product niche) |

If you can hit *at least one* of these metrics (preferably more), you have a defensible case for full‑time commitment.

## Immediate next steps (before you quit)

1. **Build a Minimal Viable Product (MVP) or Landing‑page MVP**  
   - Show enough functionality that a user can understand the value.  
   - Keep it simple; don’t wait for a “perfect” product.

2. **Run a “soft launch” or pre‑sale**  
   - Offer the first 50–100 customers a deep discount (e.g., $10/mo for 3 months).  
   - Require real payment (credit card / Stripe) to get actual conversion data.

3. **Validate pricing**  
   - A/B test $30/mo vs. $20/mo or a tiered model.  
   - Measure not just sign‑ups but *renewals*.

4. **Collect qualitative feedback**  
   - Conduct 10–15 in‑depth interviews with early adopters.  
   - Focus on *why* they signed up, what they love, and what’s missing.

5. **Track key metrics**  
   - Activation rate (first use within 3 days).  
   - Monthly churn.  
   - CAC vs. LTV.  
   - Net Promoter Score.

6. **Assess runway & risk**  
   - If you have no funding, plan to keep your job until you have at least 3–6 months of runway (i.e., paying customers or a clear path to raise).  
   - If you’re comfortable with a lean bootstrap, keep a part‑time job or a side gig.
